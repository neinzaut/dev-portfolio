Talk2DHand

Talk2DHand is a gamified learning platform designed to teach American Sign Language (ASL) to both signers and non-signers. The web application utilizes both video and audio outputs to cater to diverse learning styles. To facilitate a personalized learning journey, Talk2DHand is structured into three core modules: Learning Letters (through video or audio), Practicing Letters, and Self-Test.

For its frontend development, we utilized HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and TailwindCSS to create an intuitive and user-friendly interface, initially prototyped on Figma to ensure accessibility across different age groups. The backend development was implemented using Python, incorporating frameworks like OpenCV, MediaPipe, and TensorFlow for robust sign language recognition. Through iterative testing and model optimization, the system achieved a remarkable 97% accuracy in recognizing sign language through computer vision, validated by a professional Special Education (SPED) coordinator.
